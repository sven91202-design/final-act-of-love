My Final Act of Love

And as my final act of love, I will let you become a memory.

Not the kind that fades with time, but the kind that lingers in quiet places between unfinished sentences, in songs I can no longer listen to, in the space beside me where I once imagined you would stay.

I will stop reaching for your hand in futures that were never promised to me. I will stop building homes out of words you never meant to keep. I will stop waiting at the shore of a sea that was never coming back for me.

You were not just someone I loved. You were the person I whispered every dream to when the world was asleep. The name my heart learned before it learned caution. The beautiful mistake I would make a thousand times if it meant hearing your laugh one more time.

And that is the tragedy of it all.

I do not leave because I love you less.

I leave because I love you more than I can survive.

Because every day I stay, I lose another piece of myself trying to hold on to someone whose hands are already reaching elsewhere.

So this is my goodbye.

I will carry the weight of you for years, I know I will. I will search for your eyes in crowded rooms and hear your voice in people who sound nothing like you.

But I will love you one last time by setting you free from my hopes, from my waiting, from the version of you that only ever existed in my dreams.

And if one day you think of me, I hope it hurts just enough for you to remember that there was once a person who would have chosen you in every lifetime, every universe, every ending.

But in this one

my final act of love is learning how to live without you.
